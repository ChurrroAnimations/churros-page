            As the final year of high school approaches, many students feel a sense of apathy towards their studies, overwhelmed by the pressure to succeed. This often leads to indignation when they perceive unfair grading practices or unequal opportunities among peers. The torment of balancing college applications, exams, and social obligations can become unbearable, pushing some to react in a vehement manner when confronted with criticism. Others might make Impetuous decisions, like skipping classes, without considering the long-term consequences. As fatigue sets in, their energy becomes wretched, making it difficult to stay engaged. The once vibrant school environment can feel decrepit and uninspiring, leaving students in a fervent search for motivation. Those who find themselves in a languid state often struggle to keep up with the relentless pace of their responsibilities and may eventually capitulate to the overwhelming demands, surrendering their ambitions under the weight of stress.
